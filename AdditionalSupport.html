Whatever you or a colleague may be going through, support is always available. Below you'll find information on a range of topics along with useful external resources to help signpost you or a colleague to the right support.

Addiction support

Alcohol, drugs, gambling and other compulsive behaviours

Addiction can affect anyone, regardless of role or background. It can include alcohol, drugs, gambling, prescription medication or other compulsive behaviours. Addiction is a complex condition that can have a real impact on someone's mental, physical and emotional wellbeing.

Addiction support

What this might look like
  • Decline in work quality, reliability or concentration
  • Increased absences, lateness or withdrawn behaviour
  • Mood swings, irritability or sensitivity to feedback

Carer support

Supporting those with unpaid caring responsibilities

A carer is someone who provides unpaid support to a family member, friend or loved one who has an illness, disability, mental health condition or addiction. Caring for someone alongside work can be challenging and can have a real impact on your own health and wellbeing.

Click to see signs & support →

Carer support

What this might look like
  • Fatigue or difficulty managing work alongside caring responsibilities
  • Increased stress, anxiety or feeling overwhelmed
  • Requests for flexibility or changes to working patterns
  • Withdrawal or reduced engagement at work

Mental health

Emotional, psychological and social wellbeing

Mental health is something we all have, and just like physical health, it can change over time. It covers our emotional, psychological and social wellbeing — affecting how we think, feel and cope with day-to-day life. Mental health challenges can affect anyone at any point, from anxiety and low mood to more complex conditions.

Click to see signs & support →

Mental health

What this might look like
  • Excessive worry, anxiety or persistent low mood
  • Irritability, anger or becoming withdrawn
  • Difficulty concentrating, remembering things or making decisions
  • Withdrawal from social activities or struggling to cope with daily tasks
